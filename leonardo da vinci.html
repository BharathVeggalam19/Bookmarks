1.Home:

Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519) was an Italian polymath of the Renaissance, excelling as a painter, scientist, engineer, inventor, and anatomist. He is best known for masterpieces like *Mona Lisa* and *The Last Supper*. His notebooks contain groundbreaking studies in anatomy, flight, hydraulics, and mechanics. Though many of his inventions were never built, his visionary ideas influenced future technologies. Leonardo's curiosity and interdisciplinary approach made him one of history’s greatest geniuses.

Leonardo is world-famous for his paintings, especially the Mona Lisa, known for her mysterious smile, and The Last Supper, a masterful depiction of emotion and composition. However, his talents went far beyond art.

2.Features:

1. Mona Lisa (Painting):

Why it’s famous: The mysterious smile, realistic detail, and emotional depth make it the most iconic portrait in the world. Where it is: Louvre Museum, Paris.
Fact: The eyes seem to follow you no matter where you stand!

2.The Last Supper (Mural):

Why it’s famous: A masterpiece of composition and storytelling, showing the moment Jesus announces his betrayal. Where it is: Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie, Milan.
Fun fact: It’s painted on a wall, not canvas, and has undergone major restorations due to damage over time.

3. Vitruvian Man (Drawing):

Why it’s famous: It represents the perfect human body proportions, blending art, math, and science. Where it is: Gallerie dell'Accademia, Venice (not always on display due to its fragility).
Fun fact: It’s named after the Roman architect Vitruvius, whose work inspired Leonardo.
